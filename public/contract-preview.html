PRESSURE-TREATED WOOD INFORMATION

Pressure-treated (PT) lumber is a wood product that has been infused with chemical preservatives under pressure to resist rot, decay, and insect damage. It is commonly used in outdoor construction applications including decks, pergolas, screen porches, and structural framing. Purchaser acknowledges and agrees to the following characteristics of pressure-treated wood:

  • Natural Cracking (Checking): Pressure-treated wood is kiln-dried or air-dried after treatment. As the wood dries and acclimates to its environment, it will naturally crack along the grain. These cracks, known as "checks," are a normal and expected characteristic of pressure-treated lumber and are not a defect. Checking does not affect the structural integrity of the lumber.
  • Warping and Twisting: Individual pieces of pressure-treated lumber may warp, cup, bow, or twist over time as the wood dries and responds to changes in temperature and humidity. This is a natural characteristic of wood and is not considered a defect. Builder will use best practices to minimize the visual impact of warping, but cannot guarantee that individual boards will remain perfectly straight after installation.
  • Color Variation: Pressure-treated wood is typically greenish-brown or tan in color at the time of installation due to the chemical treatment process. Over time, untreated wood will weather to a silver-gray color if left unfinished. Color variation between individual boards is normal and expected. Staining or painting can be applied to achieve a more uniform appearance, but Builder cannot guarantee uniform color absorption due to the natural variation in wood grain and treatment levels.
  • Knots and Grain Variation: Pressure-treated lumber is a natural material and will contain knots, grain variations, and other natural characteristics. These are not defects and are expected in structural and finish-grade lumber used in outdoor construction.
  • Shrinkage: As pressure-treated wood dries after installation, it will shrink. This may cause gaps between decking boards to widen over time, and fasteners may slightly protrude or "pop" as the wood shrinks around them. This is normal and not considered a warranty item. Deck boards should be installed with appropriate spacing to accommodate shrinkage.
  • Chemical Treatment: Current pressure-treated lumber uses copper-based preservatives (such as MCA or CA-C) that are safer for residential use than older formulations. However, Purchaser should be aware that pressure-treated wood should not be used in applications where it will be in direct contact with food, and hands should be washed after extended handling of PT lumber. Builder recommends sealing or painting all cut ends of PT lumber to maximize longevity.
  • Maintenance: To maximize the lifespan and appearance of pressure-treated wood, Builder recommends applying a penetrating water-repellent sealer or exterior stain within 6 to 12 months of installation, once the wood has fully dried. Periodic reapplication (every 1 to 3 years) is recommended. Failure to maintain the wood is not a warranty item and will not be covered under Builder's one-year warranty.
  • Not a Structural Defect: None of the above-described characteristics — checking, warping, color variation, knots, shrinkage, or grain variation — shall be considered a defect in materials or workmanship under the warranty provided in this CONTRACT. Warranty claims related solely to these natural wood characteristics will not be honored.

UNFORESEEN SITE CONDITIONS POLICY

Ebony Outdoor Living and All-In-One Solutions (collectively, "Builder") strive to provide accurate and complete project estimates based on the information available at the time of contract execution. However, construction projects are subject to conditions that cannot always be anticipated before work commences. This policy describes how unforeseen site conditions are handled and the Purchaser's responsibilities related thereto.

  • Definition of Unforeseen Site Conditions: Unforeseen site conditions are physical conditions at the project site that differ materially from those indicated in the contract documents, or that are of an unusual nature, not ordinarily encountered and generally recognized as inherent in the type of work described in this contract. Examples include, but are not limited to: subsurface rock, unstable soil, unmarked underground utilities, buried debris or concrete, unexpected root systems, undisclosed fill material, high groundwater, existing structural deficiencies, concealed rot or pest damage, and non-compliant prior construction.
  • Purchaser Disclosure Obligation: Purchaser represents and warrants that, to Purchaser's knowledge, all material site conditions have been disclosed to Builder prior to execution of this contract. Purchaser is responsible for providing accurate information regarding the location of underground utilities, irrigation systems, septic systems, underground tanks, and any other subsurface improvements. Builder shall not be liable for damage to any undisclosed underground improvements.
  • Discovery and Notification: If Builder encounters conditions at the project site that appear to be unforeseen site conditions, Builder will stop work in the affected area and notify Purchaser promptly. Builder will provide Purchaser with a description of the condition encountered, a preliminary assessment of the impact on the project, and a proposed written addendum for any additional work required.
  • Additional Cost and Time: Work required to address unforeseen site conditions is not included in the original TOTAL CONTRACT SUM. Any additional work required shall be priced separately and presented to Purchaser in a written addendum. Builder shall not proceed with additional work to address unforeseen site conditions without a signed written addendum from Purchaser. If Purchaser elects not to authorize the additional work, Builder reserves the right to suspend or terminate the project, and Purchaser shall be responsible for payment for all work completed to the point of suspension or termination, plus any demobilization costs.
  • Permit and Code Compliance: If unforeseen site conditions require modifications to the project design or scope to achieve code compliance, such modifications shall be addressed by written addendum and may result in additional cost and time. Builder shall not be penalized for delays caused by permit revisions or additional inspections required as a result of unforeseen site conditions.
  • Footing Depth Adjustments: Building codes require footings to extend below the frost line and into stable, load-bearing soil. If soil conditions at the project site require footings to be excavated deeper than originally planned, this additional excavation and concrete will be billed at Builder's standard unit rate for footing extensions. Purchaser will be notified and must approve additional footing costs before work proceeds.
  • Existing Structure Interface: Where the new construction interfaces with the existing home structure (e.g., ledger attachment, tie-ins to existing rooflines, connections to existing electrical or drainage systems), concealed conditions may be discovered that require additional work. Examples include: rot or pest damage discovered behind siding or sheathing, out-of-plumb or out-of-level existing construction, non-standard framing, and existing water intrusion. Such conditions are unforeseen and shall be addressed by written addendum.
  • No Guarantee of Pre-existing Conditions: Builder makes no warranty with respect to the condition of any pre-existing structure, system, or improvement at the project site. Builder's work under this contract does not include inspection, repair, or warranty of any pre-existing conditions unless specifically listed in the Scope of Work.

Purchaser acknowledges that the construction industry regularly encounters unforeseen conditions and that this policy is a fair and reasonable allocation of risk between the parties. By signing below, Purchaser agrees to the terms of this Unforeseen Site Conditions Policy.
